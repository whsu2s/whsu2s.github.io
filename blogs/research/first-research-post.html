Neurological Disorder Analysis Using StyleGAN

Posted on 28 April, 2025

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) [1], introduced in 2014, are deep learning models that can generate realistic synthetic data by learning from real examples. StyleGAN [2], developed by NVIDIA, is a powerful variant that controls visual features at multiple levels, from broad structure to fine details, making it useful when high-quality and interpretable images are needed.

In this study [3], we used StyleGAN to model disease progression in MRI scans. By learning how healthy and diseased images differ, the model could simulate plausible future changes, offering insights into how conditions might evolve over time. We trained the StyleGAN model on MRI scans of both healthy controls and patients with multiple sclerosis (MS). By manipulating images in the model's latent space, the study successfully simulated MS progression, observing changes like brain volume loss and increased lesion size. This demonstrates the model's potential for identifying imaging markers and gaining further insight into the relationship between brain atrophy and the advancement of MS.

While GANs have become less popular recently due to the rise of diffusion models and transformers, they remain valuable tools for image generation and editing.
